About

I am a deep learning researcher at Tel Aviv University working on theoretical and empirical foundations of spectral bias and optimization dynamics, with applications to detection of AI-generated content. My background is in applied mathematics and electrical engineering (signal processing).

  • B.Sc. Applied Mathematics, Tel Aviv University (2020–2024).
  • M.Sc. Electrical Engineering, Tel Aviv University (2024–present).

Research

My work centers around the interaction between representation and optimization in the frequency domain:
  • Spectral bias: characterizing frequency-dependent learning rates and inductive bias.
  • Optimization dynamics: how training trajectories amplify/attenuate spectral components.
  • Frequency-aware mechanisms: architectures/losses/regularizers that increase sensitivity to high-frequency artifacts.
  • Applications: detection of AI-generated data in audio and images, emphasizing generalization to unseen generators.
